The Corporate Trustee

Fiduciary oversight of the Trust and the family of operating companies beneath it.

Howell Holdings is a Delaware limited liability company that serves as Corporate Trustee for The Howell Legacy Group, a Delaware Statutory Trust. The Corporate Trustee holds fiduciary responsibility for the Trust’s assets and the operating structure beneath it.

The work is straightforward: act in the interests of the trust’s beneficiaries, hold the operating structure to its standards, and preserve the family’s capital across generations.

Trustee succession is governed by the Trust Declaration. The Corporate Trustee role passes within the family, on terms designed to keep the office in family hands across generations.

Corporate Hierarchy & Governance

How the Trust, the Trustee, and the operating companies fit together.

01

Trust Structure

The Howell Legacy Group is a Delaware Statutory Trust. Howell Holdings serves as its Corporate Trustee, holding fiduciary responsibility for the Trust’s assets and the operating structure beneath it.

02

Corporate Hierarchy

A multi-tier structure separating the Trust, the Corporate Trustee, the operational holding company, the primary operating company, and the family of operating businesses. Each layer has a defined role; together they provide the asset protection, tax efficiency, and governance flexibility the mandate requires.

03

Management

John Shelton Howell III serves as Managing Trustee and Manager. Trustee succession is defined in the Trust Declaration.

04

Operating Divisions

The operating businesses span technology and patents (AITCH Technologies), software platforms (AITCH Platforms), real estate (AITCH Land Group), music rights (AITCH IP Holdings), management services (52nd Street Capital Group), and the Nevada-jurisdiction investment platform (Nevada Investment Holdings). The three independent foundations stand outside the operating structure.
